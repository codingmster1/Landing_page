Wouldn't having a degree be better?

Having a degree can be better. But it's not like becoming a doctor. For the most part, employers would rather you have the ability to actually have job ready skills. School can benefit you if they have a good Computer Science program but if you can't apply it to the workplace it's futile. There's also so many online resources to lean on to learn how to code/program.

Don't you have to be smart to learn?

Don't beat yourself up just yet. Like anything, it takes practice and persistence. It's good to start off small and with coding languages that are easy to understand. HTML and CSS to name a few. They say if you can print the words "Hello World" you're already on the right track.
